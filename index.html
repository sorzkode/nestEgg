How the nestEgg Calculator Works

üìä Calculation Methodology

1. Salary Projection

Your salary is projected year-by-year using compound growth:

Salary(year) = Current Salary √ó (1 + Annual Increase)^years

Example: $50,000 salary with 2% annual increase becomes $51,000 in year 1, $52,020 in year 2, etc.

2. Annual Contributions

Each year, contributions are calculated based on your projected salary:

Employee Contribution = Salary √ó Your Contribution % Employer Match = Salary √ó Employer Match % Total Contribution = Employee + Employer

Example: At $50,000 salary with 9% employee and 3% employer match = $4,500 + $1,500 = $6,000/year

3. Investment Growth

Your account balance grows annually based on your expected rate of return:

Interest = (Previous Balance + This Year's Contributions) √ó Rate of Return

Example: $100,000 balance + $6,000 contributions with 6% return = $6,360 growth

4. Year-by-Year Accumulation

The calculator runs this process for each year until retirement:

  1. Calculate your salary for that year
  2. Calculate contributions based on that salary
  3. Calculate investment growth on your balance
  4. Add contributions and growth to your balance
  5. Repeat for the next year

5. Inflation Adjustment

The "Purchasing Power" value shows what your final amount would be worth in today's dollars:

Real Value = Final Amount / (1 + Inflation Rate)^years

Example: $1,000,000 in 30 years with 3% inflation = $412,000 in today's purchasing power

üìà What You See in Results

  • Total at Retirement: Your projected account balance (nominal dollars)
  • Initial Savings: What you started with
  • Your Contributions: Sum of all your contributions over the years
  • Employer Contributions: Sum of all employer match contributions
  • Investment Growth: Total returns from your investments
  • Purchasing Power: What the total is worth in today's dollars
